## Why Women Need a Different Health Checkup

While a standard full body checkup covers common conditions like diabetes, liver, and kidney health — women have additional hormonal, reproductive, and nutritional needs that make certain tests particularly important.

Thyroid disorders, iron-deficiency anaemia, Vitamin D deficiency, and PCOS are among the most commonly **under-diagnosed** conditions in Indian women. An annual health checkup tailored to your life stage can catch these before they cause visible symptoms.

## Essential Tests for Women — By Age Group

### Women in Their 20s–30s

This is typically when PCOS, thyroid issues, and anaemia first emerge.

- **CBC (Complete Blood Count)** — Screens for anaemia, extremely common in Indian women due to menstrual blood loss and dietary iron deficiency
- **Thyroid Profile (TSH, T3, T4)** — Hypothyroidism is 5–8x more common in women than men; symptoms mimic stress and fatigue
- **Fasting Glucose + HbA1c** — Early diabetes screening, especially with PCOS
- **Iron Studies (Serum Iron, Ferritin, TIBC)** — Essential for menstruating women
- **Vitamin D Total** — Deficiency is found in >70% of Indian women
- **Vitamin B12** — Deficiency common in vegetarians, vegetarian women especially

### Women in Their 40s

Perimenopause can begin in the early 40s, causing hormonal fluctuations.

*All of the above, plus:*

- **Lipid Profile** — Oestrogen protects the heart before menopause; risk rises in the 40s
- **Fasting Blood Sugar / HbA1c** — Type 2 diabetes risk increases significantly
- **Liver Function Test (LFT)** — Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) is rising in Indian women
- **Kidney Function Test (KFT)** — Urinary tract infections (UTIs) increase with hormonal changes

### Women Above 50

*All of the above, plus:*

- **Calcium + Phosphorus** — Bone density decreases sharply after menopause due to oestrogen loss; early detection of calcium deficiency is critical
- **Urine Analysis** — Urinary incontinence and infections increase post-menopause
- **TSH (Thyroid)** — Thyroid changes are common in post-menopausal women
